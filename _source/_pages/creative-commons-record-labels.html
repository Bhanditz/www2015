8bitpeoples
8Bitpeoples are a chiptune powerhouse whose deep and continuously growing discography includes releases from Anamanaguchi, Animal Style, Coleco Music, and many more.

Alpinechic
Swiss-based Alpinechic specialize in post-punk and electronic music releases, utilizing CC-licenses to release singles as promotional tools for albums.

CASH Music
CASH Music is a non-profit that works on projects and releases with a diverse array of artists and labels. Much more than a label, CASH builds open-source tools and services to help artists and music organizations.

Fading Ways
Founded in Toronto, Fading Ways use CC-licenses to help fuel physical sales and promote their releases. In operation since 1996, they became one of the first music labels to use CC licenses in 2004.

A collection of netlabels distributing free and open music online. Most, although not all, of the collection is released under CC licenses.

Krayola Records
CC-licensed ambient, drone, noise, and electronica music featuring "draw your own covers" that can be simply and easily reproduced.

Loca
One of the first labels to utilize CC-licenses on their releases, Loca specializes in electronic and post-rock music.

Magnatune
Magnatune releases a broad range of music under various CC licenses and popularized the now common practice of selling commercial licensing permissions outside the original CC license.

Bad Panda Records
A new label on the CC-music scene, Bad Panda releases a free CC BY-NC-SA licensed song in to the commons every Monday.

OnClassical
Classical music netlabel offering free-to-download MP3s w/ WAV files and licensing easily available for purchase.

Quote Unquote
Quote Unquote is a netlabel focused on punk, ska, and garage music that actively seek donations for their free, CC-licensed, downloads.

RCRD LBL
RCRD LBL, a subsidiary of Downtown Music, is an online network of indie labels that posts new music daily - select releases are offered under a variety of CC licenses.

Records on Ribs
Records on Ribs use CC-licenses catalog-wide, facilitating over 70,000 downloads in a little over two years. They have created an open-source software tool, Ribcage, that aims to make setting up your own record label simple and tech-friendly.

River Rat Records
London-based River Rat Records releases all their music under a CC BY-NC-ND license to enable sharing. RRR also runs a recording studio in North London and uses sustainable materials for its physical releases.

Rock Proper
Chicago-based Rock Proper focuses on indie-rock and alt-country releases - all music is released under a CC BY-NC-SA license.

Urlyd
Danish recording label Urlyd focuses on electronic music and videos and was the first label worldwide to release a CC-licensed album with full support from a collecting society, KODA.

Beatpick
London-based Beatpick releases all its artists' music under a CC BY-NC-SA license while simultaneously pursuing commercial licensing deals.

Vosotros
An L.A.-based record label that explores new methods of music distribution/publication, including the use of CC licenses to encourage sharing and collaboration. Vosotros is music for you-all.

Silenced
Belgian netlabel Silenced focuses on electronic music releases and is an officially recognized nonprofit. Silenced also organizes events and acts as a booking agency.

Luxus Artica [http://luxusarctica.wordpress.com/] Ambient and electronic netlabel Luxus Artica uses a CC BY-NC-ND license to enable sharing on its releases. Broadly works with artists on advertising, networking, identity, and design.

blocsonic Netlabel blocSonic has a tiered approach to finding and distributing CC-licensed music - originals (blocSonic found artists), Xtended Editions (re-releases), and netBlocs (full featured digital compilations).
Resting Bell [http://www.restingbell.net/] Berlin-based Resting Bell releases their entire catalog under a CC BY-NC-ND license.
Peppermill Records [http://www.peppermillrecords.com] Canadian netlabel Peppermill Records focuses on collaborative projects between musicians, releasing these works under a CC BY-NC-ND license.

ccMixter ccMixter is an online remix community focused on enabling derivative musical works on a large scale. Originally sponsored by Creative Commons, ccMixter is now sponsored by ArtisTech Media.
Indaba Music Indaba Music is a hub for online collaboration between musicians. Their digital recording tools enable artists to share loops and songs under the CC license of their choosing as well as pick from a large pool of professional CC-licensed samples and loops. Indaba is well known for their artists remix contests that often use CC licenses to facilitate user-end remix promotion.
Jamendo Jamendo allows artists to publish their CC-licensed music for free, providing direct access to their active community. Select artists also have work included in Jamendo PRO, the site's music licensing arm.

SectionZ SectionZ is an eletcronic music community that allows artists to upload their works under the full suite of CC licenses.
SoundCloud Soundcloud is a music sharing community that allows artists to upload their works under the full suite of CC licenses. Their set of tools integrate nicely across the web, with adoptions from well known artists and labels.
Free Music Archive The Free Music Archive is a project from WFMU that focuses on aggregating and curating high quality, freely licensed content - the majority of which is CC-licensed. Currators include KEXP, dublab, CBC Radio 3, All Tomorrow's Parties, and Primavera Sound.
BandCamp BandCamp allows artists to build simple and straight forward profiles to sell and distribute their music in a range of musical formats. Artists are able to indicate CC-licenses on these releases and, as a result, easily sell CC-licensed material.
Simuze Simuze is a music sharing community that allows members to upload their works under a CC license of their choosing.
ArtistServer ArtistServer is a music sharing community that allows users to upload their works under the CC license of their choosing and features exemplary search filtering by license.
