What about license proliferation?

Creative Commons provides a standard set of licenses suitable to a wide variety of uses and situations, reducing the need for custom licenses. By doing this, CC prevents license proliferation, which threatens the commons by enabling the creation of separate pools of incompatible material.

Though not everyone wants to adopt a CC license, we can't resolve those differences by using copyright to restrict modifications on the license text itself. Many other licenses already exist for those who wish to choose other terms. CC encourages potential adopters to consider using our standard licenses, which have a long history and a thriving user base, rather than creating new, incompatible modified versions. A license that is modified cannot be called a CC license, so while someone may create a new license based on our text, the risk of confusion or mistaken adoption is lowered.
