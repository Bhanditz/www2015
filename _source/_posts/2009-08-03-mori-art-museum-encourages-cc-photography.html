The Mori Art Museum in Tokyo wanted a new way to engage with their visitors and decided they should encourage them to come take photos at some of their exhibitions, so they picked their current exhibition of Ai Wewei's work titled "According to what?" The exhibit features many large objects and installations and runs through November 8th, so if you're in Tokyo definitely stop by.

What's great

Ai Weiwei is one the most

Exhibition Information

- The latest exhibition at Mori Art Museum, in which CC license is applied,
is Ai Wewei's solo exhibition, titled "According to what?"
from 25 July to 8 November 2009.

- Ai Weiwei is one of the most famous artists in China
as well as an artistic resistant to Chinese government showing
his opinions through his artworks. Now his blog is shut down
by Chinese government as he wrote too much claims in it.

FYI) Exhibition website
http://www.mori.art.museum/english/contents/aiweiwei/index.html

- MAM judged this exhibition was the best chance to let visitors take
photos, as Ai Weiwei was likely to say OK to adopt Creative Commons,
and solo exhibition is much easier to clear the copyright issue

- His artworks are mainly huge objects and installations,
so we need not worry about "illegal duplication" of his artworks
due to allowing people to take photos

How to apply CC to the exhibition

- CCJP prepared the draft CC license agreement between the museum
and the artist. Most conservative license CC BY-NC-ND 2.1 Japan
was chosen to reduce risks.

- Every artwork is allowed to be taken picture
(Operation might be much harder if the museum had excluded several artworks)

- Put the notice at the entrance and the flyers to give away to visitors,
showing the instructions people have to follow when take pictures and
use them later, including CC introduction.

- MAM prohibited these actions;
No movie shooting
No tripod
No flash

Attached please find the files of agreement and instructions.

Some Troubles we face now

- Bad manner; as it's almost the first time Japanese people can
take picture in a museum, people tend to be too noisy
taking memorial pictures in the museum, not artwork pictures.

- Noise when people take picture using mobile phone cameras
